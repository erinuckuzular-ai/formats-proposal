01 · Where this comes from

The work has no home

Every few weeks a show needs something built. A call sheet that works on a phone on shoot day. A quiz that plays properly on screen. A way to see who's drowning in edits. There's no one whose job it is to do any of it.

It can land on me, and it's some of the work I enjoy most, but right now it has no owner and no home. This is a proposal to give it one.

02
02 · The gap

Every show solves the same problems alone

One goes off-site and improvises a call sheet. One goes abroad and the handover gets cobbled together. One wants a quiz and there's no asset for it.

Knowledge and tools don't travel between pods. And when a workflow breaks, there's no one to take it to. No shared layer underneath the slate, and no one whose job is to look forward.

On the engineers

A producer who builds. Not a second engineering team.

Engineers cost a lot, aren't producers, and need a lot of tending. They're brilliant for big, analytical, scaling builds, work I'd happily do alongside them. This is the other thing: fast, format-first tools from inside the show, with a producer's instinct for what the show and the talent need.

07 · The objections, met

Straight answers

Q Can't all producers just learn this?+

They can learn pieces, but they're at capacity delivering shows. Spread across everyone it happens slowly, inconsistently, and nobody owns it. One person who builds once and shares scales faster than the whole team learning in fragments.

Q Why not just use the engineers?+

Engineers cost a lot of money, aren't producers, and need a lot of tending. They're the right call for big, analytical, scaling builds, and I'd happily work alongside them on those. But the day-to-day, format-first tools belong with someone inside the show, at a speed and cost that doesn't fit an engineering roadmap.

Q Is it really a role, with enough work?+

The backlog is the proof. I'm already doing this in the cracks of another job, and the shipped-plus-queued list is longer than one person's spare time.

Q Who covers the show?+

I keep producing one. Never off the floor.

Q Is it worth it?+

Yes. In hours returned, spreadsheets retired, tools we don't buy, and an edge competitors don't have. There's a calculator further down to put real numbers on it.

09 · Room to grow

The title says formats & innovations on purpose

AI is the main tool in the kit right now. But the remit is staying ahead: building relationships with the AI companies so we're early not late, testing new tools and formats before anyone else, and formats that have nothing to do with AI at all. We start focused and let it earn its expansion.
